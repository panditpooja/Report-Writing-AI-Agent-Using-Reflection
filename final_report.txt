### 1. Executive Summary  

Biosphere 2 has transitioned from a pioneering ecological experiment into a global hub for interdisciplinary science, technology, and entrepreneurship. Central to this transformation is **Jeff Larsen**, the **Innovation Officer**. Larsen’s role is to translate the facility’s scientific discoveries into commercial and societal impact by fostering partnerships, leading AI‑driven platforms, and nurturing a vibrant student‑entrepreneur ecosystem. His work has positioned Biosphere 2 as a leader in autonomous research systems, digital twins, and “Science as a Service” models, thereby extending the facility’s influence beyond academia into industry and public policy.

### 2. Background of Biosphere 2  

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Construction** | 1987‑1991, 3.14‑acre sealed greenhouse containing rainforest, desert, ocean, mangrove, savanna, and farm. |
| **Current Status** | Operated by the University of Arizona; hosts research, education, and industry partnerships. |
| **Mission** | To understand Earth’s biomes and develop scalable solutions for sustainability and planetary colonization. |

### 5. Major Projects & Initiatives  

#### 5.1 Biosphere 3 – AI‑Powered Science as a Service  
- **Vision**: A cloud‑based platform that aggregates sensor data, simulation models, and AI analytics to provide researchers with real‑time insights and predictive capabilities.  
- **Larsen’s Role**: Product Manager, guiding architecture, user experience, and integration with existing Biosphere 2 data streams. (Staff profile)  
- **Impact**: Enables researchers to run complex ecological simulations without on‑site computational resources, accelerating discovery cycles.  

#### 5.2 B2Twin – Digital Twin of Biosphere 2  
- **Concept**: A virtual replica of Biosphere 2 that mirrors its physical state, allowing scenario testing, predictive maintenance, and educational outreach.  
- **Program**: Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) that brings together students, faculty, and industry partners to develop the digital twin. (B2Twin page)  
- **Larsen’s Contribution**: Coordinates cross‑disciplinary teams, secures funding, and ensures alignment with the facility’s research goals. (B2Twin page)  

#### 5.3 AI & Autonomous Research Systems  
- **Autonomous Monitoring**: AI‑driven sensors and robotics monitor environmental parameters, detect anomalies, and automate routine tasks.  
- **Research Applications**: Supports studies on climate change, ecosystem dynamics, and human‑environment interactions.  
- **Larsen’s Role**: Oversees integration of autonomous technologies into the research workflow, ensuring data quality and operational reliability. (Staff profile)  

#### 5.4 Student Workforce Development & Spin‑Off Support  
- **Program**: Training students in AI, XR, robotics, and digital twins; providing mentorship, seed funding, and access to Biosphere 2’s data and infrastructure. (Staff profile)  
- **Outcome**: Several student‑led projects have been showcased at conferences and have led to early‑stage start‑ups applying Biosphere 2 research to real‑world problems. (Staff profile)
